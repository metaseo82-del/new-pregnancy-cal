How this AP Chem score calculator works

The calculator converts your multiple-choice performance and total free-response points into weighted section scores. Each section represents 50% of the overall AP Chemistry exam, so the final estimate is shown on a 100-point composite scale.

The compression setting changes estimated score boundaries. That gives students, tutors, and publishers a way to model a stricter or more forgiving projected curve without changing the official exam structure.

AP Chemistry calculator FAQ

Is this an official AP score?+
No. It is a projection tool based on exam weighting and estimated score bands. Official AP scores are set after College Board equating and scoring processes.
What does compression level mean?+
Compression changes the spacing of the projected score thresholds. Higher compression makes bands tighter and slightly more forgiving. Lower compression spreads them out and makes the estimate stricter.
Can I embed this on my website?+
Yes. This is a standalone HTML file with embedded CSS and JavaScript, so it can be dropped into most websites or static hosting setups.
Is the calculator mobile responsive?+
Yes. The layout collapses cleanly for tablets and phones, with touch-friendly inputs and buttons.
